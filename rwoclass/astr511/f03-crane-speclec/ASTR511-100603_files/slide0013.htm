Difficulties with fiber-fed systems
• Added complexity
• Problems associated with Focal Ratio Degradation (FRD)
    - microbending in optical fibers leads to a net lowering of the f/ratio of light transmitted to the spectrograph
    - uncertainty of spectrograph input f/ratio can make choice of collimator difficult
    - since the collimator ends up being  faster, the camera in turn has to be equivalently faster. Ugh.
• Lower efficiency (throughput) for an individual spectrum, although this can be made up for by collecting lots of spectra simultaneously

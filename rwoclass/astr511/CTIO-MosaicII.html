The Mosaic II CCD Imager
on the CTIO Blanco 4m

CTIO Support Scientists:
Alistair Walker, Chris Smith, Knut Olsen

A clone of the 8K Mosaic Imager presently in use on the KPNO Mayall and 0.9-m telescopes has been commisssioned at CTIO, replacing the BTC Mosaic Imager at the Blanco 4-m prime focus. The 8 SITe 2Kx4K CCDs for Mosaic II were tested in the Tucson CCD Lab. After assembly of the focal plane, the CCDs were taken to CTIO and installed in the dewar, and full system tests begun. We have modified the prime focus area, strengthening the pedestal and enlargening the cage doors. Mosaic II was put on the Blanco 4-m for engineering tests on July 12-14 1999, and has been scheduled for general visitor use for subsequent semesters.

DOCUMENTATION: The CTIO version of the MOSAIC MANUAL contains all of the general Mosaic information (in common with the KPNO Mosaic, Mosaic I) together with details specific to CTIO. (This CTIO manual is also available in postscript as a gzipped, tar file, about 700 kBytes). Given that Mosaic II is a clone of Mosaic I, much of the extensive documenatation available for the latter is directly relevant. You can find this at the NOAO Mosaic Imager homepage (NOTE however that the manual available there contains information specific to the KPNO Mosaic I, which may NOT apply to the CTIO Mosaic II). Information on aspects that are different between the two installations (eg CCD properties) will appear here as it becomes available. One important scheduling difference is that at CTIO Mosaic II will not be offered at any telescope apart from the 4-m prime focus, and will be the only optical imager scheduled at that telescope.

The 16-channel mode (2 amps per chip), was tested and works, and is now the default mode (as of Feb 2001) .For the July and August 1999 engineering runs the instrument was used in 8-channel mode. Initial pictures taken during these runs are available here.

SKY RATES
(last measured 21 Oct 2000)

To aid in planning observations, we've measured sky rates with the Mosaic II for moonless sky. Note that these are given in electrons/sec and are the average for all 8 CCDs. Given that several of the CCDs have low full wells (above), one must be especially careful with the I filter to not limit the dynamic range of the observations (e.g., a 20 min exposure only leaves you a usable range of 10000 counts).
